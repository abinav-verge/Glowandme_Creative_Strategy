## Level of Awareness
**Classification: Problem Aware → Solution Aware (transitioning)**
She is deeply aware of her problem — she sees the wrinkles, she's bothered by them, she's seeking solutions actively. She has moved past the "Problem Aware" stage in that she knows solutions exist (tretinoin, Botox, microneedling, face taping, HRT), but she has not yet settled on the right one for her, and she is skeptical of the category as a whole.
She is not "Product Aware" because she hasn't found a product she trusts or has consistent evidence for. She is not "Most Aware" because she is not yet ready to buy — she is still researching, comparing, and building the internal conviction needed to act.
The most powerful marketing approach meets her exactly at the Solution Aware stage: acknowledge that she's been researching, validate that most solutions have disappointed her, and then introduce a mechanism of action she hasn't yet encountered or dismissed — with social proof from women who look and sound exactly like her.
- "I've not tried taping. I feel like I'm too far gone for that unfortunately."
Insight: She is Solution Aware enough to have heard of face taping but not yet confident it applies to her situation. The "too far gone" belief is a barrier to solution adoption, not evidence of Unaware status.
- "I've been researching red light therapy and the concept seems really promising. But at the same time I'm skeptical."
Insight: Classic Solution Aware behavior — active research paired with protective skepticism. She wants to believe, but she needs more evidence to cross the trust threshold.
